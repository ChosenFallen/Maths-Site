## Source 1: UK National Curriculum (KS3, Years 7-9)

### Overview
- All schools must cover every maths skill listed in the KS3 national maths curriculum by end of Year 9
- Curriculum broken into 6 mathematical themes: **Number, Algebra, Ratio, Geometry, Measure, Statistics**

### Year 7
- Developing understanding of fractions and ratios as multiplicative relationships
- Working with coordinates across four quadrants
- Rotations, reflections, enlargements, translations
- Perimeter and area applications

### Year 8
- Continuing study of ratio, proportion, rates of change with more challenge
- Multiplicative relationships, percentages as multiplicative relationships
- Understanding lines, perimeter, volume, area problem solving

### Year 9
- Similar broad topics as Years 7-8 but with increased complexity and depth
- Integration of earlier learning into more sophisticated applications

## Source 2: Primary School Curriculum (Year 5-6)

### Year 5 Topics

**Addition & Subtraction:**
- Add/subtract whole numbers with 4+ digits
- Formal written methods (columnar addition/subtraction)
- Mental addition/subtraction with increasingly large numbers
- Rounding to check answers
- Multi-step problems in contexts

**Multiplication & Division:**
- Multiply numbers up to 4 digits by 1-2 digit number
- Formal written method (long multiplication)
- Divide numbers up to 4 digits by 1-digit number (short division)
- Multiples and factors
- Prime numbers, prime factors, composite numbers

**Fractions:**
- Compare/order fractions with related denominators
- Equivalent fractions (visual representations, tenths, hundredths)
- Mixed numbers and improper fractions
- Add/subtract fractions with same denominator or related denominators

### Year 6 Topics

**Addition & Subtraction:**
- Practice with larger numbers
- Columnar addition/subtraction mastery
- Short and long multiplication
- Short and long division

**Multiplication & Division:**
- Multiply multi-digit numbers (up to 4 digits) by 2-digit whole number
- Divide numbers up to 4 digits by 2-digit whole number
- Interpret remainders (whole numbers, fractions, rounding)

**Fractions:**
- Add/subtract fractions with different denominators and mixed numbers
- Equivalent fractions concept
- Common factors to simplify fractions
- Common multiples to express fractions with same denominator

## Source 3: GCSE Maths Topic Distribution by Grade

### Overall Structure
- **Foundation Tier:** Can achieve grades 3-5
- **Higher Tier:** Can achieve grades 4-9
- Grade descriptors give "expected mid-point performance at grades 2, 5 and 8"

### Topic Weighting by Tier
- **Foundation:** Greater focus on Number and Ratio topics
- **Higher:** Greater focus on Algebra and Geometry/Measure

### Topic Difficulty Distribution

**Foundation Tier Focus (Grades 1-5):**
- Number (higher weighting)
- Ratio, Proportion, Rates of Change (higher weighting)
- Basic Algebra
- Geometry and Measure (basic)
- Simple Statistics

**Higher Tier Focus (Grades 4-9):**
- Algebra (higher weighting)
- Geometry and Measure (more complex)
- Number (sophisticated)
- Statistics

### Crossover Content
- Some questions appear on both tiers to ensure fairness between tiers

## Source 4: Advanced Topic Difficulty Analysis

### Specific Topic Grade Ranges

**Algebraic Fractions:**
- Primarily Higher Tier content
- More advanced content, indicating higher grades (6-9 range)

**Inequalities:**
- Linear inequalities: More basic, covered in lower grades
- Quadratic inequalities: Advanced, for higher grades (7-9)

**Quadratics:**
- Basic quadratic equations: Grades 6-7
- Advanced sections (factorising, formula, completing square): Grades 7-9
- "Later sections are probably not worth practicing unless aiming for Grade 8 or 9"

**Trigonometry:**
- Grade 4-6 students will struggle with majority of this topic
- First learning objective could be easy marks
- "Later sections are probably not worth practicing unless aiming for Grade 8 or 9"

**Surds:**
- Advanced algebraic topic, likely Grade 7-9 range

**Sequences:**
- Linear sequences: Grades 4-6
- Quadratic sequences: Grades 6-8
- Geometric sequences: Grades 6-8

## Source 5: Educational Institution Frameworks

### School Curriculum Maps & Parent Guides
Various schools and educational platforms have created year-by-year curriculum maps showing topic progression. Common observations:

**Grade Level Progression Pattern:**
1. **Grades 1-3:** Foundation arithmetic, basic number operations
2. **Grades 4-5:** Extended number operations, introduction to algebra concepts, basic geometry
3. **Grades 6-7:** Complex algebra, early abstract concepts, statistical reasoning
4. **Grades 8-9:** Advanced algebra, sophisticated geometry, complex problem solving

## Key Insights from Research

1. **No Single Grade Descriptor Per Topic:** The GCSE system uses grade descriptors at 2, 5, and 8 as midpoints, not strict grade boundaries for each topic

2. **Tier-Based Rather Than Grade-Based:** Much of the grading system is based on Foundation vs Higher tier rather than individual grades

3. **Topic Progression:** Topics build from foundation (Year 5-6) → secondary core (Years 7-8) → advanced (Year 9-GCSE)

4. **Crossover Content:** Most topics appear at both lower and higher grade levels, but with varying complexity

5. **Grade-Specific Difficulty:** Within a topic area, basic versions appear at lower grades, while advanced versions appear at higher grades (e.g., linear inequalities vs quadratic inequalities)

6. **GCSE Alignment:** GCSE spans grades 1-9, with Foundation tier ending at grade 5 and Higher tier starting at grade 4

## Notes for Future Mapping

When mapping our worksheets to grades:
- Consider both topic name AND difficulty level (easy/normal/hard)
- Recognize that topics don't fit into single grades but rather grade ranges
- Higher tier topics (algebraic fractions, advanced trigonometry) naturally belong in grades 7-9
- Foundation tier topics naturally belong in grades 1-6
- Mid-range topics (quadratics, basic sequences) span 6-8
- Foundation topics (basic arithmetic, simple fractions) start from grade 1-2
