## Overview
The "Super 8s" are essential skills that students should master at each stage of their mathematics education. This document collates all topics and sub-skills from Mr Barton's Year 7, Year 8, Year 9, GCSE Foundation (Non-Calculator, Calculator, 1-Mark Wonders), and GCSE Higher (Non-Calculator, Calculator, 1-Mark Wonders) Essential Skills resources.

- **Year 7 Skills**: Foundation and core mathematical thinking (40+ skills across 13 areas)
- **Year 8 Skills**: Build on Year 7, introduce more complex operations (35+ skills across 12 areas)
  - Examples: multiplying fractions, solving equations with brackets, standard form
- **Year 9 Skills**: Advanced skills preparing for GCSE (37+ skills across 12 areas)
  - Examples: double bracket expansion, graph equations, Pythagoras' theorem, reverse percentages
- **GCSE Foundation (Non-Calculator)**: Essential non-calculator exam skills (47+ skills across 9 areas)
  - Examples: prime factor decomposition, fraction conversions, algebra basics, geometry essentials
- **GCSE Foundation (Calculator)**: Essential calculator exam skills (49+ skills across 10 areas)
  - Examples: compound interest, trigonometry, circles, best value problems
- **GCSE Foundation 1-Mark Wonders**: High-frequency quick-fire questions (51+ skills across 13 areas)
  - Examples: digit value, rounding, factors/primes, coordinate reading, shape classification
- **GCSE Higher (Non-Calculator)**: Advanced non-calculator exam skills for Grades 9-7 (69+ skills across 20 areas)
  - Examples: surds, simultaneous equations, circle theorems, trigonometric rules, algebraic proof
- **GCSE Higher (Calculator)**: Advanced calculator exam skills for Grades 9-7 (45+ skills across 9 areas)
  - Examples: non-linear simultaneous equations, conditional probability, upper/lower bounds, iteration
- **GCSE Higher 1-Mark Wonders**: High-frequency quick-fire questions (43+ skills across 12 areas)
  - Examples: fractional indices, standard form, bounds, exact trig values, vectors
- **Together**: Create a coherent pathway from KS3 (Years 7-9) through complete GCSE (Foundation + Higher) coverage
