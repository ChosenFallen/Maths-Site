## 1. NUMBER (16 subtopics) - Code: N

### Integers & Place Value
- **N1**: Integer place value and ordering
- **N2**: Rounding to specified accuracy
- **N3**: Factors, multiples, HCF, LCM
- **N4**: Prime factorization

### Fractions, Decimals & Percentages
- **N5**: Fractions - equivalent, simplifying, ordering
- **N6**: Fractions - add, subtract, multiply, divide
- **N7**: Decimals - operations and equivalence
- **N8**: Percentages - find, calculate, reverse percentages
- **N9**: Conversions between fractions, decimals, percentages

### Powers & Roots
- **N10**: Indices (positive, negative, fractional)
- **N11**: Standard form (ordinary and standard notation)
- **N12**: Surds and simplification

### Ratios & Proportions
- **N13**: Ratio and scaling
- **N14**: Direct proportion
- **N15**: Inverse proportion

### Estimations & Bounds
- **N16**: Upper and lower bounds, error intervals

- **A4**: Factorising expressions

## 3. RATIO, PROPORTION & RATES OF CHANGE (16 subtopics) - Code: R

- **R3**: Combining ratios

### Percentages & Growth
- **R10**: Percentage increase and decrease

- **R13**: Compound interest

### Rates of Change

## Foundation Tier Topics

All topics listed above are covered at Foundation tier, but with simpler applications and calculations. Foundation tier focuses on:
- Single-step and two-step calculations
- Basic algebraic manipulation
- Standard geometric shapes and properties
- Simpler probability and statistics
- No complex simultaneous equations or trigonometric equations

## Important Curriculum Notes

### What is NOT in GCSE Mathematics

❌ **Formal Calculus** - No derivatives or integrals taught in formal calculus notation
- **Instead covered**: Gradient analysis, rate of change, optimization through algebra

❌ **Complex Numbers** - Not part of GCSE

❌ **Matrices** - Not part of standard GCSE (some boards may offer as extension)

❌ **Vectors** - Not formally part of 1MA1 specification (though some papers may include vector geometry)

### All Exam Boards Are Equivalent

The following exam boards all follow the **same national curriculum** for GCSE Mathematics:
- Pearson Edexcel (1MA1)
- AQA (8300)
- OCR (H560)
- Walsall
- City & Guilds

This means the 91 subtopics and their depth are consistent across all boards.

## Skill Development Framework

### Procedural Skills (40%)
- Computational accuracy
- Application of formulae
- Manipulation of expressions
- Standard procedures

### Conceptual Understanding (35%)
- Why methods work
- Connections between topics
- Justification and reasoning
- Problem interpretation

### Problem-Solving & Reasoning (25%)
- Multi-step problems
- Unfamiliar contexts
- Mathematical communication
- Proof and verification

## Cross-Topic Links

Key connections that frequently appear in papers:

1. **Algebra + Geometry**: Circle equations, trigonometric graphs
2. **Number + Ratio**: Proportional reasoning, percentage applications
3. **Algebra + Probability**: Probability tree diagrams, expected value
4. **Geometry + Trigonometry**: Finding angles and sides in 3D shapes
5. **Statistics + Ratio**: Interpreting scaled data, sampling ratios
6. **Functions + Rates of Change**: Gradient analysis of curves
